# User Testing Protocol — Blackjack Card Counting Tutor

**COMP 395: AI and Learning Technologies | Spring 2026**
Minimum 4 external participants required per SDD scope.

---

## Facilitator Notes

- Sessions run approximately 30–45 minutes each.
- Do not help the participant unless they are completely stuck. Note when they struggle.
- Record observations in the **Observation Log** (separate sheet) alongside each task.
- All participants must complete the Pre-Session Questionnaire before touching the app.
- All participants must complete the Exit Questionnaire before leaving.

---

## Part 2 — Moderated Task Scenarios

*Read each task aloud to the participant. Do not show them this sheet. Observe and record without guiding.*

---

### Task 1 — Account Creation & First Login

> "Please create an account and log in to the app."

**Watch for:**
- Does the participant find the registration flow without prompting?
- Any confusion about email/password requirements?
- Does the app handle errors (wrong password, existing email) gracefully and clearly?

**Pass criteria:** Participant reaches the Home / Level Select screen within 3 minutes without facilitator help.

---

### Task 2 — Explore the Home Screen

> "Without starting a level yet, take a moment to look at this screen. Tell me what you think you can do here."

**Watch for:**
- Does the participant understand what the locked levels mean?
- Do they notice the "About" section?
- Can they identify the level progression model (1 → 2 → 3 → 4)?

**Note any elements that cause confusion.**

---

### Task 3 — Complete Level 1 (Basic Strategy)

> "Start Level 1 and play through it. Read anything the tutor says."

**Watch for:**
- Does the participant understand the Hit / Stand / Double / Split buttons without explanation?
- Do they read the LLM tutor feedback, or ignore it?
- Are they aware of their score tracker?
- Do they understand what it means to complete the level (≥70% correct)?
- Any confusion about when the shoe ends / level ends?

---

### Task 4 — Interact with the Tutor Panel

> "Ask the tutor a question about something you just saw or something you're confused about."

**Watch for:**
- Does the participant know how to type into the tutor panel?
- Does the LLM response feel relevant and helpful to them?
- Do they attempt a follow-up question?

---

### Task 5 — Attempt a Locked Level

> "Try to open Level 3."

**Watch for:**
- Is the locked state clearly communicated visually (grayed out / lock icon)?
- Does the error message tell them what they need to do next?
- Does the participant feel frustrated or does the message feel fair?

---

### Task 6 — Level 2 Count Classification Drill

> "Start Level 2 and complete the drill."

**Watch for:**
- Does the participant understand they are classifying counts (positive / negative / neutral), not playing a hand?
- Do they understand the Hi-Lo +1 / 0 / −1 assignment before they start?
- Is the drill speed comfortable, too fast, or too slow?

---

### Task 7 — Exit the App Mid-Session

> "Imagine you need to stop and come back later. Close this level and log out."

**Watch for:**
- Can the participant find a way to exit the current game session back to the home screen?
- Can they find the logout action?
- After logging back in, is their progress preserved?

**Pass criteria:** Participant logs out and, upon re-login, sees their completed levels still marked as done.
